
# Darcy-Weisbach Law

$$
k = \frac{h}{T} \cdot \frac{\mu \cdot L}{\Delta p \cdot A}
$$

---

## Variables

| Symbol | Description | Unit (SI) |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| h | liquid columsn height | $m$ |
| T | integration time | s |
| $\mu$ | fluid viscosity | Pa·s |
| L | sample length (thickness) | m |
| $\Delta p$ | differential pressure | N/$m^2$ |

## Example

h = 10 mm <br>
T = 10 s <br>
mu = 6.4e-4 Pa.s <br>
L = 10 mm <br>
$\Delta p$ = 100 mbar <br>
k = 6e-13 m2
